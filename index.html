Prayers of the Prophet ﷺ
Allah - beginning with the name of - the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful
It has been narrated that the Messenger of (ﷺ) said:
A PRAYER RECOMMENDED TO BE READ 100 TIMES EVERY DAY
لاَ إِلَهَ إِلاَّ اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لاَ شَرِيكَ لَهُ، لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ
"There is no god but Allah, alone, without any partner. To Him belongs sovereignty, and to Him belongs all praise, and He is over all things competent."
If one says these one hundred times a day, he will be given the reward of freeing ten slaves, one hundred good deeds will be written for him, one hundred of his sins will be erased, and it will be a shield for him against Satan until the evening. No one can surpass him in reward except someone who says it more than him.
(Bukhari, Bad'u'l-Halk, 11)
For Spiritual Wealth
Hasan Basri (may Allah sanctify his secret) used to make the following supplication to Allah Almighty:
اللَّهُمَّ أَغْنِنِي بِالْفَقْرِ إِلَيْكَ وَلاَ تُفْقِرْنِي بِالاِسْتِغْنَاءِ عَنْكَ
"O Allah! Enrich me by making me dependent on You, and do not make me poor by making me independent of You!"
(Baqillani,s. 107)
During Calamity
On one occasion, the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ)
said: "If a Muslim upon whom a calamity has befallen says, Allah will remove that calamity from him and surely give him something better in return:"
إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ. اللَّهُمَّ أَجِرْنِي فِي مُصِيبَتِي وَأَخْلِفْ لِي خَيْرًا مِنْهَا
"Indeed, to Allah we belong, and to Him we shall return. O Allah, reward me in my calamity and replace it with something better for me!"
(Muslim, Janaiz, 3)
Salatul Istikhara (Guidance)
The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ)
used to teach us to pray Istikhara (guidance prayer) for every matter, just as he would teach us a Surah from the Qur'an. He said:
"When one of you intends to do something, let him pray two rak'ahs apart from the obligatory prayers, then make the following dua:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْتَخِيرُكَ بِعِلْمِكَ وَأَسْتَقْدِرُكَ بِقُدْرَتِكَ وَأَسْأَلُكَ مِنْ فَضْلِكَ الْعَظِيمِ فَإِنَّكَ تَقْدِرُ وَلاَ أَقْدِرُ وَتَعْلَمُ وَلاَ أَعْلَمُ وَأَنْتَ عَلاَّمُ الْغُيُوبِ اللَّهُمَّ إِنْ كُنْتَ تَعْلَمُ أَنَّ هَذَا الأَمْرَ خَيْرٌ لِي فِي دِينِي وَمَعَاشِي وَعَاقِبَةِ أَمْرِي – فِي عَاجِلِ أَمْرِي وَآجِلِهِ – فَاقْدُرْهُ لِي وَإِنْ كُنْتَ تَعْلَمُ أَنَّ هَذَا الأَمْرَ شَرٌّ لِي فِي دِينِي وَمَعَاشِي وَعَاقِبَةِ أَمْرِي – فِي عَاجِلِ أَمْرِي وَآجِلِهِ – فَاصْرِفْهُ عَنِّي وَاصْرِفْنِي عَنْهُ وَاقْدُرْ لِي الْخَيْرَ حَيْثُ كَانَ ثُمَّ رَضِّنِي بِهِ
'O Allah, I seek Your counsel by Your knowledge, and I seek Your power by Your power, and I ask You from Your immense Favor. For You are able and I am not, and You know and I do not, and You are the Knower of the unseen. O Allah, if You know that this matter (and here he would mention his specific matter) is good for me in my religion, my livelihood, and the outcome of my affair – in its immediate and future consequences – then decree it for me. And if You know that this matter is bad for me in my religion, my livelihood, and the outcome of my affair – in its immediate and future consequences – then turn it away from me and turn me away from it, and decree for me the good wherever it may be and then make me content with it.'
During the dua, (mention what you wish for this matter) he would mention it.
(Bukhari, Da'avat, 48)
Leaving the House
It is narrated that when the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ)
left his house, he would surely turn his face to the sky and make the following dua:
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أَضِلَّ أَوْ أُضَلَّ أَوْ أَزِلَّ أَوْ أُزَلَّ أَوْ أَظْلِمَ أَوْ أُظْلَمَ أَوْ أَجْهَلَ أَوْ يُجْهَلَ عَلَيَّ
"In the Name of Allah, I have put my trust in Allah. O Allah! I seek refuge in You from going astray or being led astray, from slipping or being made to slip, from oppressing or being oppressed, from acting ignorantly or being treated ignorantly."
(Abu Dawud, Adab, 102-103; Tirmidhi, Da'avat, 35)
Prayer for Need
The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ)
advised anyone who has a need to perform two rak'ahs of prayer, then make dua to Allah. He said:
"Whoever has a need for Allah or for a human being, let him first perform ablution, do it beautifully, then pray two rak'ahs, then praise Allah Almighty, and then make the following dua:
لاَ إِلَهَ إِلاَّ اللَّهُ الْحَلِيمُ الْكَرِيمُ. سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ. الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ. اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ مُوجِبَاتِ رَحْمَتِكَ وَعَزَائِمَ مَغْفِرَتِكَ وَالْغَنِيمَةَ مِنْ كُلِّ بِرٍّ وَالسَّلاَمَةَ مِنْ كُلِّ إِثْمٍ. لاَ تَدَعْ لِي ذَنْبًا إِلاَّ غَفَرْتَهُ وَلاَ هَمًّا إِلاَّ فَرَّجْتَهُ وَلاَ حَاجَةً هِيَ لَكَ رِضًا إِلاَّ قَضَيْتَهَا لِي
"There is no god but Allah, the Forbearing, the Generous. Glory be to Allah, Lord of the Magnificent Throne. All praise belongs to Allah, Lord of the worlds. O Allah! I ask You for the means of Your mercy and the firm resolve of Your forgiveness, and the spoils of every good deed, and safety from every sin. Do not leave any sin for me except that You forgive it, nor any worry except that You relieve it, nor any need that is pleasing to You except that You fulfil it for me."
(Ibn Majah, Iqamah, 189; Tirmidhi, Witr, 17)
Protection from Evil
Our Prophet (ﷺ)advised seeking refuge in Allah for protection from evils, saying:
أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
"Whoever alights in a place and then says:
'I seek refuge in the perfect words (verses, attributes, and names) of Allah from the evil of what He has created.'
nothing will harm him until he departs from that place."
(Muslim, Dhikr, 54-55)
Prayer Before Sleeping
Our beloved Prophet ((ﷺ), when he wanted to sleep, would lie on his right side, place his right hand under his right cheek, and then make the following dua:
اللَّهُمَّ أَسْلَمْتُ نَفْسِي إِلَيْكَ وَوَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِي إِلَيْكَ وَفَوَّضْتُ أَمْرِي إِلَيْكَ وَأَلْجَأْتُ ظَهْرِي إِلَيْكَ رَغْبَةً وَرَهْبَةً إِلَيْكَ لاَ مَلْجَأَ وَلاَ مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلاَّ إِلَيْكَ آمَنْتُ بِكِتَابِكَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلْتَ وَنَبِيِّكَ الَّذِي أَرْسَلْتَ
"O Allah! I have surrendered myself to You, I have entrusted my affair to You, I have turned my face to You. Desiring Your pleasure and fearing Your punishment, I have learned my back on You, I have sought refuge in You. There is no refuge and no escape from You except to You. I believe in Your Book which You revealed and Your Prophet whom You sent."
(Bukhari, Da'avat, 7)
During Salah
Rasulullah (peace be upon him) always recited it during Salah Before Salam.
اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي مَا قَدَّمْتُ وَمَا أَخَّرْتُ وَمَا أَسْرَرْتُ وَمَا أَعْلَنْتُ وَمَا أَسْرَفْتُ وَمَا أَنْتَ أَعْلَمُ بِهِ مِنِّي أَنْتَ الْمُقَدِّمُ وَأَنْتَ الْمُؤَخِّرُ لاَ إِلَهَ إِلاَّ أَنْتَ
Ya Allah, forgive me for the sins I have committed and the sins I have delayed, and the sins I have hidden and the sins I have revealed, and the sins that exceeded my limits and the sins that You know better than me. You are the One Who brings forward and the One Who delays, there is no god except You.
[HR. Muslim: 771]
Refuge from Hypocrisy
Our Prophet (ﷺ)sought refuge in Allah as follows:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الشِّقَاقِ وَالنِّفَاقِ وَسُوءِ الأَخْلاقِ
"O Allah! I seek refuge in You from dissension, from hypocrisy, and from bad manners."
(Abu Dawud, Salat, 367)
Witr Prayer
Hz. Hasan (may Allah be pleased with him) reported that his esteemed grandfather taught him the following dua to recite in Witr prayer:
اللَّهُمَّ اهْدِنِي فِيمَنْ هَدَيْتَ وَعَافِنِي فِيمَنْ عَافَيْتَ وَتَوَلَّنِي فِيمَنْ تَوَلَّيْتَ وَبَارِكْ لِي فِيمَا أَعْطَيْتَ وَقِنِي شَرَّ مَا قَضَيْتَ فَإِنَّكَ تَقْضِي وَلاَ يُقْضَى عَلَيْكَ وَإِنَّهُ لاَ يَذِلُّ مَنْ وَالَيْتَ، تَبَارَكْتَ رَبَّنَا وَتَعَالَيْتَ
"O Allah, include me among those whom You have guided on the straight path. Grant me well-being along with those You have granted well-being. Take me as a friend along with those You have taken as friends. Bless for me that You have given. Protect me from the evil of what You have decreed. For indeed, You decree and none can decree against You. And surely, he whom You take as a friend is not humiliated. You are blessed, our Lord, and exalted. Truly, Your blessings and grace are eternal and abundant, and Your glory is immense!"
(Tirmidhi, Vitir, 10)
Against Bad Luck
In the presence of the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), ill omens were mentioned. Thereupon, he said:
"The best of it is not to interpret it as bad luck. Bad luck should not make any Muslim give up his endeavour. When any of you sees something, he dislikes, he should make the following dua:
اللَّهُمَّ لاَ يَأْتِي بِالْحَسَنَاتِ إِلاَّ أَنْتَ وَلاَ يَدْفَعُ السَّيِّئَاتِ إِلاَّ أَنْتَ وَلاَ حَوْلَ وَلاَ قُوَّةَ إِلاَّ بِكَ
'O Allah! Only You bring good deeds; only You ward off evil deeds. There is no might and no power except with Your help.'"
(Abu Dawud, Tib, 24)
Unite Hearts
Our Prophet (ﷺ), always warned his companions against falling into disbelief and sins that lead to disbelief, and he would advise them to make the following dua:
اللَّهُمَّ أَلِّفْ بَيْنَ قُلُوبِنَا وَأَصْلِحْ ذَاتَ بَيْنِنَا وَاهْدِنَا سُبُلَ السَّلاَمِ وَنَجِّنَا مِنَ الظُّلُمَاتِ إِلَى النُّورِ وَجَنِّبْنَا الْفَوَاحِشَ مَا ظَهَرَ مِنْهَا وَمَا بَطَنَ وَبَارِكْ لَنَا فِي أَسْمَاعِنَا وَأَبْصَارِنَا وَقُلُوبِنَا وَأَزْوَاجِنَا وَذُرِّيَّاتِنَا وَتُبْ عَلَيْنَا إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ التَّوَّابُ الرَّحِيمُ وَاجْعَلْنَا شَاكِرِينَ لِنِعْمَتِكَ مُثْنِينَ بِهَا قَابِلِيهَا وَأَتِمَّهَا عَلَيْنَا
"O Allah! Unite our hearts, rectify our affairs, guide us to the paths of peace, and save us from darkness into light. Keep us away from immoralities, both apparent and hidden. Bless our hearing, our sight, our hearts, our spouses, and our offspring. Accept our repentance, for You are the Acceptor of Repentance, the Most Merciful. And make us grateful for Your blessings, praising them, accepting them, and complete them upon us!"
(Abu Dawud, Salat, 178)
Protection from Calamities (Abu Dawud)
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ التَّرَدِّي، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْهَدْمِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْغَرَقِ وَالْهَرَمِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ يَتَخَبَّطَنِي الشَّيْطَانُ عِنْدَ الْمَوْتِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أَمُوتَ فِي سَبِيلِكَ مُدْبِرًا، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أَمُوتَ لَدِيغًا
"O Allah! I seek refuge in You from falling from a height. O Allah! I seek refuge in You from being crushed by a building, from falling into a chasm. O Allah! I seek refuge in You from drowning, from fire, and from (excessive) old age. I seek refuge in You from Satan striking me during death. I seek refuge in You from dying while turning away from Your path. I seek refuge in You from dying from poisoning."
(Abu Dawud, Musnad, II, 92, nr. 1552)
Protection from Calamities (Nesai)
Our Prophet (ﷺ), sought refuge in Allah as follows:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ التَّرَدِّي وَالْهَدْمِ وَالْغَرَقِ وَالْحَرِيقِ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ يَتَخَبَّطَنِي الشَّيْطَانُ عِنْدَ الْمَوْتِ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أَمُوتَ فِي سَبِيلِكَ مُدْبِرًا وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أَمُوتَ لَدِيغًا
"O Allah! I seek refuge in You from falling from a high place, from being crushed/falling under a collapse, from drowning, from burning. And I seek refuge in You from being tormented by Satan at the time of death. And I seek refuge in You from dying while fleeing from Your path. And I seek refuge in You from dying from a snake bite."
(Nesai, as-Sunenu'l-Kübra, Istiaze, 65, No:7972)
Dua of Abdullah ibn Mas'ud
Abdullah ibn-i Mas'ud (may Allah be pleased with him) once recited Surah An-Nisa and made a dua. Seeing this, the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) said three times, “Behold! Whatever you ask for will be given to you!” Abdullah ibn-i Mas'ud's dua was as follows:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ إِيمَانًا لاَ يَرْتَدُّ وَنَعِيمًا لاَ يَنْفَدُ وَمُرَافَقَةَ نَبِيِّكَ مُحَمَّدٍ فِي أَعْلَى جَنَّةِ الْخُلْدِ
"O Allah! I ask You for faith that does not falter, and blessings that do not end, and the companionship of Your Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) in the highest gardens of eternity."
(Ahmed, I, 445; Hakim, III, 358/5386)
Entry and Exit
Our Lord, in the Holy Qur'an, has asked our Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) to make the following dua:
رَبِّ أَدْخِلْنِي مُدْخَلَ صِدْقٍ وَأَخْرِجْنِي مُخْرَجَ صِدْقٍ وَاجْعَلْ لِي مِنْ لَدُنْكَ سُلْطَانًا نَصِيرًا
"...My Lord! Grant me to enter with a truthful entry and to exit with a truthful exit, and grant me from Your presence an assisting authority."
(Isra Surah 80. Verse)
WHENEVER THIS DUA IS RECITED
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لاَ يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلاَ فِي السَّمَاءِ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
Imam-ı Rabbânî Hazretleri (may Allah sanctify his secret) stated that during a journey, a fire broke out in a caravanserai. He said: "Today, we see that a calamity will befall this place and spread to everyone. Our friends should tell each other. Everyone should recite these duas repeatedly. Because whoever recites these duas, by the grace of Allah Almighty, he and his property are protected. Within a short time, a fire broke out in the caravanserai. Despite all human efforts, the fire could not be extinguished. Many perished and lost their property. It is narrated that when Mevlana Abdülmümin Lahorî's property burned, he barely saved his life. Imam-ı Rabbânî Hazretleri came to his presence and asked, "Did anyone tell me to recite the necessary duas?" He replied, "No, master." Imam-ı Rabbânî Hazretleri admonished his students who did not inform him and said, "Whoever recites this dua, he and his property will be saved."
Blessed Blessings
O Allah! MAKE THE BLESSINGS YOU HAVE GIVEN BLESSED
It has been narrated that the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), made the following dua:
اللَّهُمَّ اهْدِنِي فِيمَنْ هَدَيْتَ، وَعَافِنِي فِيمَنْ عَافَيْتَ، وَتَوَلَّنِي فِيمَنْ تَوَلَّيْتَ، وَبَارِكْ لِي فِيمَا أَعْطَيْتَ، وَقِنِي شَرَّ مَا قَضَيْتَ، إِنَّكَ تَقْضِي وَلاَ يُقْضَى عَلَيْكَ، وَإِنَّهُ لاَ يَذِلُّ مَنْ وَالَيْتَ، وَلاَ يَعِزُّ مَنْ عَادَيْتَ، تَبَارَكْتَ رَبَّنَا وَتَعَالَيْتَ
"O Allah! Guide me among those whom You have guided and grant me well-being among those whom You have granted well-being and take me as a friend among those whom You have taken as friends, and bless for me that You have given, and protect me from the evil of what You have decreed. Indeed, You decree and none can decree against You, and surely, he whom You befriend is not humiliated, and he whom You oppose is not honored. Blessed are You, our Lord, and Exalted!"
Thanking Someone
According to a narration from Usama ibn Zayd (may Allah be pleased with him); our Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) said: "Whoever has good done to him, if he says to the one who did the good: 'May Allah reward you with good' (Allah, may He reward you with good) he will have thanked and prayed for him in the best way."
جَزَاكَ اللَّهُ خَيْرًا
(Tirmidhi, Birr, 87)

PROPHET ADAM'S (PEACE BE UPON HIM) SUPPLICATION
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ بِحَقِّ مُحَمَّدٍ وَآلِ مُحَمَّدٍ سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ عَمِلْتُ سُوءًا وَظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لاَ يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلاَّ أَنْتَ وَتُبْ عَلَيَّ إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ التَّوَّابُ الرَّحِيمُ
"O Allah! I ask You by the right of Muhammad and the family of Muhammad. Glory be to You, O Allah, and with Your praise. I have done wrong and wronged myself, so forgive me, for indeed no one forgives sins except You. Accept my repentance, for indeed You are the Acceptor of Repentance, the Most Merciful."
Blessing in Provisions
It has been narrated that the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), made the following dua:
اللَّهُمَّ بَارِكْ لَنَا فِي مِدِّنَا وَصَاعِنَا
"O Allah! Bless our products that we measure and weigh."
(Bukhari, Da'avat, 42)
Removing Anger
Our Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him), when our mother Aisha (may Allah be pleased with her) was angry, advised her to make the following dua:
اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي ذَنْبِي وَأَذْهِبْ غَيْظَ قَلْبِي وَأَجِرْنِي مِنْ مُضِلاَّتِ الْفِتَنِ
"O Allah! Forgive my sin, remove the anger from my heart, and protect me from misleading trials."
(Tabarani, el-Mu'cemü'l-Kebir, XXIII, 338, No: 785)
Refuge from 4 Things
Our Prophet (ﷺ), sought refuge in Allah from these four things
أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنْ جَهْدِ الْبَلاءِ، وَدَرَكِ الشَّقَاءِ، وَسُوءِ الْقَضَاءِ، وَشَمَاتَةِ الأَعْدَاءِ
"I seek refuge in Allah from severe affliction, from reaching wretchedness, from a bad decree, and from the malicious joy of enemies."
(Bukhari, Da'awat 28; Dawud 28; Qadar 13; Muslim, Dhikr 53. Also, Nesai, Istiaze 34, 35)
Prayer Before Sleeping (Wudu)
The Messenger of Allah (peace and blessings be upon him) advised to perform ablution before sleeping and recite the following supplication:
اللَّهُمَّ أَسْلَمْتُ نَفْسِي إِلَيْكَ، وَوَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِي إِلَيْكَ، وَفَوَّضْتُ أَمْرِي إِلَيْكَ، وَأَلْجَأْتُ ظَهْرِي إِلَيْكَ، رَغْبَةً وَرَهْبَةً إِلَيْكَ، لاَ مَلْجَأَ وَلاَ مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلاَّ إِلَيْكَ، آمَنْتُ بِكِتَابِكَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلْتَ، وَبِنَبِيِّكَ الَّذِي أَرْسَلْتَ
"O Allah! I have surrendered myself to You. I have turned my face to You. I have entrusted my affair to You. I have learned my back on You, out of desire for You and fear of You. There is no refuge and no escape from You except to You. I believe in Your Book which You revealed and Your Prophet whom You sent."
(Bukhari, Da'awat, 6, 7, 9; Muslim, Dhikr and Dua, 56-57; Abu Dawud, Adab, 98; Tirmidhi, Da'awat 16; Ibn Majah, Dua, 15)

PASTE CONTENT
Morning & Evening
Morning extends from the middle of the night until sunrise.
1. Protection from Shaytan
بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمْنِ الرَّحِيْمِ وَ لاَ حَوْلَ وَ لاَ قُوَّةَ اِلأَبِللهِ العَلِىِّ العَظِيمِ
In the name of Allah, the Beneficent, the Merciful. There is no power and no strength except with Allah, the Most High, the Supreme.
2. Increase Faith (41 Times)
يَا حَىُّ يَا قَيُوْمُ لا إِلهَ إِلاَ أَنْتَ
Yã Hayyu Yà Qayyumu la ilaha illa anta.
O Ever-Living One! O Eternally Self-Sustaining One! There is no one worthy of worship except You.
3. Protection from insanity (3 Times)
سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ العَظِيْمِ وَ بِحَمْدِهِ
SubhānAllahil 'Azim wa bi Hamdih.
Glory be to Allah, the Supreme, and for Him is all praise.
4. Ayat al-Kursi (Once)
اللَّهُ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّوْمُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَّلَا نَوْمٌ ۭ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمٰوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۭ مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِنْدَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۭ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۚ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمٰوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۚ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ
Allah, there is no god but He, the Living, the Everlasting. Slumber seizes Him not, neither sleep; to Him is all that is in the heavens and the earth. Who is there that shall intercede with Him save by His leave? He knows what lies before them and what is after them, and they comprehend not anything of His knowledge save such as He wills. His Throne comprises the heavens and earth; the preserving of them oppresses Him not; He is the All-High, the All-Glorious.
5. Surah Ghafir (First 3 Verses)
بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمِنِ الرَّحِيْمِ
حمٓ (١) تَنزِيلُ ٱلْكِتَٰبِ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ ٱلْعَزِيزِ ٱلْعَلِيمِ (٢) غَافِرِ ٱلذَّنبِ وَقَابِلِ ٱلتَّوْبِ شَدِيدِ ٱلْعِقَابِ ذِى ٱلطَّوْلِ ۖ لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ إِلَيْهِ ٱلْمَصِيرُ
Ha-Mim. The revelation of the Book is from Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Knowing. The Forgiver of sin, acceptor of repentance, severe in punishment, owner of abundance. There is no deity except Him; to Him is the destination.
6. Protection from snakes (3 Times)
أَعْوْذُ بِكَلِمْتِ اللهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from the evil of what He has created.
7. Protection against poison (3 Times)
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَذِي لَا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْئٌ فِى الأَرْضِ وَلاَ فِى السَّمَاءِ وَ هُوَ السَّمِيْعُ العَلِيمُ
In the name of Allah, by Whose Name nothing harms on earth or in the heavens; and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing.

رَضِيتُ بِالله رَبًّا وَ بِالإِسلامِ دِينًا وَ بِسَيِّدِنَا وَ مَوْلانَا مُحَمَّدٍ صَلَّى اللهُ تَعَالَى عَلَيْهِ وَ سَلَّمَ نَبِيًّا وَ رَسُوْلا
I am content with Allah as my Lord, Islam as my religion, and with Muhammad as His Prophet and Messenger.
8. Protection from all ills (Once)
فَسُبْحَٰنَ ٱللَّهِ حِينَ تُمْسُونَ وَحِينَ تُصْبِحُونَ (١٧) وَلَهُ ٱلْحَمْدُ فِى ٱلسَّمَٰوَاتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَعَشِيًّا وَحِينَ تُظْهِرُونَ (١٨) يُخْرِجُ ٱلْحَىَّ مِنَ ٱلْمَيِّتِ وَيُخْرِجُ ٱلْمَيِّتَ مِنَ ٱلْحَىِّ وَيُحْىِ ٱلْأَرْضَ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا ۚ وَكَذَٰلِكَ تُخْرَجُونَ
So glory be to Allah, both in your evening hour and in your morning hour. His is the praise in the heavens and in the earth, and at little before the setting of sun and when you enter the noon. He brings forth the living from the dead and brings forth the dead from the living, and gives life to the earth after its death. And in like manner you shall be brought forth.
9. Protection of faith & wealth (3 Times)
بِسْمِ اللهِ عَلَى دِيْنِيْ بِسْمِ اللهِ عَلَى نَفْسِىْ وَ وُلْدِيْ وَ اَهْلِى وَ مَالِىْ
In the Name of Allah, I seek blessings and assistance in my religion, myself, my children, my family, and my wealth.
10. Gratitude (Once)
اللُّهُمّ مَا أَصْبَحَ [أَمْسَى] بِىْ مِنْ نَعْمَةٍ آَوْ بِاَحَدٍ مِنْ خَلْقِكَ فَمِنْكَ وَحْدَك لاَ شَرِيْكَ لَكَ فَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ وَلَكَ الشُّكْرُ . لاَ اِلَهَ اِلاَّ آنَّتَ سُبْحَانَكَ اِنِّى كُنْتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِيْنَ
O Allah, whatever blessings have come to me or to someone from Your creation in the morning [or evening], then it is from You, the One Who has no partner, for You is all praise and gratitude. There is no one worthy of worship except You, surely I am from the wrongdoers.
11. Protection from Shaytan (Once)
بِسْمِ اللهِ ذِىْ الْشَّأْنِ عَظِيْمِ الْبُرْهَانِ شَدِيْدِ السُّلْطَانِ مَا شَآءَ اللهُ كَانَ اَعُوْذُ بِاللهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّحِيْمِ
Allah - beginning with the name of -, the Sultan of supreme honor, splendor, and authority. Everything is in accordance with the determination and will of Allah, and His will is fulfilled. I seek refuge in Allah from Shaytan, the cursed.
12. Safety from Hellfire (4 Times)
اللَّهُمْ إِنِّى اَصْبَحْتُ [أُمْسَيْتُ] أُشْهِدُكَ وَ اُشْهِدُ حَمَلَةَ عَرْشِكَ وَ مَلاَئِكَتَكَ وَ جَمِيعَ خَلْقِكَ أَنَّكَ أنْتَ اللهُ لاَ اِلهَ إِلَّا آَنْتَ وَحْدَكَ لاَ شَرِيْكَ لَكَ وَ اَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُكَ وَ رَسُوْلُكَ
O Allah, I enter the morning [or evening] bearing witness before You - and before the carriers of Your Throne, all of Your angels, and all of creation - that You are God; there is no god but You alone, without partner, and that Muhammad is Your servant and messenger.
13. Day's Worship (Once)
اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ الْحَمْدُ حَمْدًا دَائِمًا مَعَ دَوَامِكَ وَ لَكَ الْحَمْدُ حَمْدًا خَالِدًا مَعَ خُلُوْدِكَ وَ لَكَ الْحَمْدُ حَمْدًا لَا مُنْتَهِيَ لَهُ دُوْنَ مَشِيْئَتِكَ وَ لَكَ الْحَمْدُ حَمْدًا لَا يُرِيدُ قَائِلُهُ إِلَّا رِضَاكَ وَ لَكَ الْحَمْدُ حَمْدًا عِنْدَ كُلِّ طَرْفَةِ عَيْنٍ وَ تَنَفُّسِ كُلَّ نَفَسٍ
O Allah for You is all praise, such a praise that is everlasting with Your permanence. And for You is all praise, such that is infinite with Your immortality. And for You is all praise, such that has no limit. And for You is all praise, such that its writer does not intend through it save Your pleasure. And for You is all praise upon every blinking of an eye and at the time of respiration of every breath.
14. For Debt & Anguish (11 Times)
الَّهُمْ إِنَّى اَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْهَمِّ وَ الْحُزْنِ وَ اَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْعَجْزِ وَ الْكَسَلِ وَ اَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنَ الجُبْنِ وَالْبُخْلِ وَ أَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنْ غَلَبَةِ الدَيْنِ وَ قَهْرِ الرِّجَالِ
O Allah, I seek refuge in You from all anxiety and grief, and I seek refuge in You from all weakness and indolence, and I seek refuge in You from cowardice and covetousness, and I seek refuge in You from overwhelming debt and from the overpowering of men.
15. Istikhara (7 Times)
اللَّهُمّ خِرْ لِى وَاخْتَرْ لِىْ وَ لا تَكِلْنِي إِلى اخْتِيَارِىْ
O Allah, choose for me that which is best, and do not entrust me to my own choice.
16. Sayyid al-Istighfar
اللُّهُمّ أَنْتَ رَبِّى لا إِلهَ إِلَّا آَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِى وَ آَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلى عَهْدِكَ وَ وَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ اَعُوْذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّمَا صَنَعْتُ اَبُوْءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَىّ وَ أَبُوْءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِيْ فَاغْفِرْ لِىْ فَاِنَّهُ لاَ يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوْبَ إِلاّ أَنْتَ
Allah, You are my Lord; there is no God but You. You created me and I am Your slave. And Your covenant and promise I uphold to the best of my ability. And I seek refuge in You from the evil of whatever I have done. I acknowledge that all my blessings are from You. And to You I bring my sins, so forgive me because no one can forgive sins but You.
17. Shield against hunger/fear (100 Times)
لاَ اِلَهَ إِلَّا الّهُ الْمَلِكُ الْحَقُّ الْمُبِيْنُ
There is no one worthy of worship except Allah, the Sovereign, the Truth, the One Who makes things clear.
